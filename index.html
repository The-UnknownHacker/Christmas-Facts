1. The First Christmas Card

The first Christmas card was created in 1843 by John Calcott Horsley in England. It was sent to Sir Henry Cole and featured a family celebrating Christmas.

2. The Origin of Santa's Red Suit

Santa Claus's iconic red suit became popular after Coca-Cola started using him in their advertisements in the 1930s. Before that, Santa was depicted in a variety of colors, including green and blue.

3. Christmas Trees Were Once Banned

In the 17th century, Christmas trees were actually banned in some places, including Puritan-controlled areas of New England, as the holiday was considered too pagan.

4. The Longest Christmas Carol

The longest Christmas carol ever written is "The Twelve Days of Christmas," which has 364 lines. That's a lot of gift-giving!

5. Christmas Is the Largest Annual Celebration

Christmas is the most widely celebrated holiday in the world. Over 2 billion people around the globe celebrate Christmas each year in various ways.
